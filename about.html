About Me

I’m James Hill, a U.S. Army veteran and student of information science and information technology. I enjoy building practical tools and websites that make it easier for people to find the information, services, and opportunities they need.

My interests sit at the intersection of digital equity, community information, and hands‑on technology. I’ve worked on projects ranging from AI‑assisted HR concepts and community resource hubs to educational games and small data‑analysis projects. I like to take complex systems and make them feel understandable and approachable.

Outside of school work, I’m a dad, maker, and 3D‑printing enthusiast who enjoys creating things that bring other people a little joy — whether that’s a board game prototype, a website, or a simple tool that solves an everyday problem.

Outside of school and building technology projects, I enjoy recreational gold panning. There’s something relaxing about being outdoors, working through the river sediment, and seeing what small treasures nature hides. It blends patience, exploration, and curiosity — qualities I bring into my academic and professional work as well.
